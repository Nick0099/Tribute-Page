Dr. Sanduk Ruit

The "God of Sight" who restored vision to over 180,000 people

Here's a timeline of Dr. Ruit's life:

  • 1954 - Born in Olangchungola, a remote village in Eastern Nepal
  • 1970s-1980s - Completed ophthalmology residency at All India Institute of Medical Sciences, Delhi
  • 1980 - Met Australian eye surgeon Fred Hollows, who became his mentor
  • 1986 - Collaborated with Hollows in Australia to develop small-incision cataract surgery using intraocular lenses
  • 1994 - Founded the Tilganga Institute of Ophthalmology in Kathmandu to provide affordable eye care
  • 1995 - Developed a low-cost intraocular lens, enabling affordable cataract surgery in over 60 countries
  • 2006 - Performed cataract surgeries and trained surgeons in North Korea after gaining special access
  • 2021 - Launched the Tej Kohli & Ruit Foundation to screen 1 million people and cure 300,000 of cataract blindness by 2026
